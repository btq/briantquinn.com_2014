Welcome

I have recently earned my PhD at NYU's Center for Neural Science investigating visual-tactile sensory integration. That is, I explored how the human brain takes disparate sensory signals from somatosensory and visual streams and creates a coherent percept. This process is necessary for establishing a sense of peri-personal space (the space on and immediately around our bodies) and for interacting with our physical environment. Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my B.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Iowa and conducted research on morphometric properties of the brain at Harvard University. For a record of my publications, see the tab above.

What's next?

I'm currently applying and interviewing for jobs in the private sector. I'm most interested in software development, research, data science, or consulting. Preferably somewhere with a fast-paced and collaborative work environment. One of the things gleaned from my doctoral work is that I much prefer to work with a collaborative group and achieve results quickly, rather than spend years in solitude conducting tedious research.

What else?

My interests focus on novel data processing methods and visualization for complex signals. I enjoy programming, analyzing data, cooking, sudoku, canoeing, and reading.
